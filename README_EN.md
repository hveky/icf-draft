## The problem

In SSU and similar rehabilitation settings, **revising and writing ICF functioning assessments** is frequent, repetitive, and format-strict work:
cross-checking ICF qualifiers and categories line by line, rewording to spec, keeping terminology consistent — time-consuming and error-prone amid the tedium.

**icf-draft aims to automate that writing**, so assessors spend their time on judgment and patients instead of shuffling text and polishing formatting.

## 🎯 Who it's for

- Rehabilitation physicians, therapists, and assessors
- SSU staff who produce or revise functioning reports under the ICF framework
- Clinical teams looking to reduce documentation burden

## ✨ Envisioned capabilities

> Directional — to be delivered incrementally as development proceeds.

- 📝 **Structured revision** — assist generating and revising assessment text against ICF categories and qualifiers.
- 🔁 **Less repetitive work** — hand the high-frequency, templated writing to automation; humans confirm and judge.
- 📑 **Consistent terms & formatting** — unify wording and layout, cutting manual review cost.
